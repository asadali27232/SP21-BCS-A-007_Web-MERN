Apples are grown in more than 90 countries across the globe, covering a total area of approximately 9 million acres.

Apples are one of the widely consumed fruits globally, and they are often ranked among the top five fruits in terms of production.

03

Apples have been cultivated for thousands of years, with records dating back to ancient civilizations like the Egyptians and Greeks.

Apple diseases pose a significant threat to orchards worldwide, impacting both the quantity and quality of fruit production.

Scab

Fungal disease affecting apple trees, characterized by dark lesions on leaves. Proper management practices, including pruning and fungicide application, are essential to protect tree health.

Learn More

Frog Eye Leaf Spot

Fungal disease affecting apple trees, characterized by circular lesions with dark centers and light halos. Vigilant management is crucial for maintaining tree health and fruit quality.

Learn More

Rust

Fungal disease in apple trees, manifests as reddish-orange pustules on leaves and can harm fruit production. Timely prevention and management are vital for tree health.

Learn More

Powdery Mildew

Fungal disease in apple trees, presents as a powdery white substance on leaves, affecting tree vigor and fruit quality. Diligent management is essential for prevention.

Learn More

Complex

Complex disease in apple trees involves simultaneous infections of two or more different diseases, requiring comprehensive management for tree health and fruit quality.

Learn More

Healty

A healthy apple leaf is characterized by its vibrant green color, smooth texture, and uniform appearance. It should be free from any discoloration, blemishes, or spots.
